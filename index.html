01 / Multi-agent automation

PO Automation Engine

A 5-agent pipeline that turns a vendor email into a verified delivery - no human re-keying in between. Built for 50–60 retail chains across 400+ delivery centres.

System architecture - PO Automation Engine
Problem

50–60 retail chains (Zepto, Blinkit, Reliance, D-Mart) sent POs by email across 400+ delivery centres. Each one was manually read, validated, and keyed into SAP - a process that took days.

What I built

A 5-agent pipeline: ingestion reads the email, validation checks against contract terms, an order-creation agent generates the SAP entry, scheduling slots delivery, and a GRN agent verifies goods received. I owned requirements, agent-boundary design, and sign-off across Sales, Supply Chain, and Finance.

Key tradeoff

Discrete agents over one end-to-end model - POs touch financial records, so a failed stage needed to be retried in isolation, not by reprocessing the whole order. Each boundary was a deliberate trust checkpoint.

Lesson

The MVP relied on predefined mappings for known PO formats. A v2 would feed exception resolutions back in, so the system becomes adaptive rather than staying rule-dependent.

Days → Minutes

PO processing time across 400+ delivery centres

02 / Enterprise analytics

Heritage Samarth & Prism

Self-serve analytics for two audiences with nothing in common: a Branch Manager checking today's revenue, and a data team surfacing farmer-level churn before it costs the business milk volume.

System architecture - Samarth + Prism
Problem

Sales performance and collection-centre health lived in disconnected reports. Leadership from Branch Manager to CEO needed real-time, role-appropriate visibility - without waiting on an analyst.

What I built

Samarth: role-based analytics governing ₹6.6 Cr in daily revenue visibility, with region drill-downs and SE performance tracking. Prism: RFM segmentation across 150+ collection centres, processing 10+ crore farmer-level rows to surface churn risk before it becomes a procurement problem.

Key tradeoff

Chose RFM over a heavier ML churn model. The business needed interpretable segments to drive procurement and field decisions - stakeholder trust mattered as much as accuracy. RFM delivered that with far fewer data requirements.

Lesson

Insight accuracy depended more on master data quality than on adding more analytics. A v2 would automate data quality checks and anomaly detection before generating recommendations - not after users noticed errors.

10+ Cr rows

farmer-level data segmented for churn risk, 150+ collection centres

03 / Geospatial · ML

Geospatial Territory Redesign

Clustering 900+ field agents across six states to find the milk volume nobody was capturing - and translating a model into a decision regional leadership would actually act on.

Analysis pipeline - territory clustering
Problem

Distributor placement had grown organically over years. No one could confidently identify which retail clusters were under-penetrated versus simply under-served by territory design.

What I built

Applied KNN and DBSCAN clustering across field-agent and retail-coverage data for 900+ agents in six states to algorithmically surface under-penetrated clusters, then built the data-backed case for new distributor placements to regional leadership.

Key tradeoff

Chose DBSCAN because retailers cluster naturally - not in uniform circles. It identifies real-density clusters while filtering outliers, producing recommendations that reflect market reality better than any fixed-radius approach would.

Lesson

Accurate recommendations alone didn't drive adoption - users needed transparent reasoning and estimated business impact before acting on territory changes. A v2 would add distributor capacity, travel time, and serviceability constraints alongside geography.

+16,000 L/day

incremental milk sales from new distributor placements backed by the model

04 / Consumer · Trust

Purity Ka Report Card

A QR code on a milk packet that lets any consumer verify the actual quality batch behind it. Conceived and shipped end-to-end: QA portal, public lookup site, and packaging integration.

System architecture - Purity Ka Report Card
Problem

Milk quality testing happened, but it was invisible to the end consumer - a trust gap with no product behind it.

What I built

Conceived and delivered the platform end-to-end: an internal QA batch-entry portal, a public batch-code lookup site with PDF download, and QR code integration directly on packaging connecting the two.

Key tradeoff

Chose a QR-enabled public web experience over an in-app feature - every consumer with a smartphone camera could instantly verify quality without downloading anything, maximising accessibility and trust at the point of purchase.

Lesson

Transparency alone doesn't build trust - context does. Consumers wanted simple explanations of what quality parameters meant, not raw lab values. A v2 would pair batch reports with freshness indicators and product education, turning verification into ongoing engagement.

consumer batch lookups, tracked as a brand trust signal
