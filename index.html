LOG / 01 Multi-agent automation

PO Automation Engine

A 5-agent pipeline that turns a vendor email into a verified delivery — no human re-typing in between.

Problem

50–60 modern retail chains — Zepto, Blinkit, Reliance, D-Mart — sent purchase orders by email across 400+ delivery centers. Every PO was manually read, validated, and keyed in, for days at a time, with entry errors baked into the process.

What I built

Architected and shipped a 5-agent AI pipeline: ingestion reads the inbound email, a validation agent checks it against contract terms, an order-creation agent generates the order, a scheduling agent slots delivery, and a GRN agent verifies goods received — each a discrete, auditable step rather than one opaque model call.

My role

Owned requirements, agent-boundary design, and stakeholder sign-off across Sales, Supply Chain, and Finance. Worked with engineering on pipeline architecture and failure handling for edge-case POs.

Result

Processing time fell from days to minutes. Manual entry errors were eliminated at the point they used to happen — entry itself.

Days → Minutes PO processing time, 400+ delivery centers
LOG / 02 Enterprise analytics

Heritage Samarth & Prism

Self-serve analytics for two very different audiences: a Branch Manager checking today's numbers, and a data team chasing farmer-level churn.

Problem

Sales performance and collection-center health lived in disconnected reports. Leadership from Branch Manager to CEO needed real-time, role-appropriate visibility without waiting on an analyst.

What I built

Samarth: a role-based analytics platform tracking real-time sales KPIs and SE performance with region drill-downs, governing ₹6.6 Cr in daily revenue visibility. Prism: applies RFM segmentation across 150+ collection centers, processing 10+ crore farmer-level rows to surface churn risk before it becomes a problem.

My role

Defined the KPI taxonomy and role-based access model, prioritized the RFM segmentation approach with the data science team, and drove adoption across branch-to-CEO user tiers.

Result

Self-serve visibility replaced manual reporting for revenue tracking and churn risk — the explicit pattern Flipkart's own JD describes wanting more of.

10+ Cr rows farmer-level data, segmented for churn risk
LOG / 03 Geospatial / ML

Geospatial Territory Redesign

Clustering 900+ field agents across six states to find the milk volume nobody was capturing.

Problem

Distributor placement had grown organically over years. Nobody could say with confidence which retail clusters were under-penetrated versus simply under-served by territory design.

What I built

Applied KNN and DBSCAN clustering across field-agent and retail-coverage data spanning 900+ agents in six states to algorithmically surface under-penetrated clusters, then used that analysis to recommend new distributor placements.

My role

Framed the business question as a clustering problem, ran the analysis, and presented the data-backed case to regional leadership — the part that actually decides whether a model becomes a decision.

Result

16,000 litres per day in incremental milk sales unlocked through new distributor placement, backed by a model leadership could see and question.

16,000 L/day incremental sales from re-territorying
LOG / 04 Consumer-facing trust

Purity Ka Report Card

A QR code on a milk packet that lets any consumer look up the actual quality batch behind it.

Problem

Milk quality testing happened, but it was invisible to the end consumer — a trust gap with no product behind it.

What I built

Conceived and delivered the platform end-to-end: an internal QA batch-entry portal, a public batch-code lookup site with PDF download, and QR code integration directly on packaging connecting the two.

My role

Owned the product end-to-end — from the original concept through QA workflow design, public-facing UX, and the packaging integration handoff with operations.

Result

Scaled to thousands of monthly lookups, with measurable consumer trust signals tracked directly by the brand team.

1000s/mo consumer batch lookups
